Disputes, Damaged Items, and Escalation Policy
1. Overview

This document outlines the procedures for handling disputes related to orders, including damaged items, incorrect deliveries, missing products, and customer dissatisfaction. It also defines escalation mechanisms when issues cannot be resolved through standard support processes.

The objective is to ensure fair resolution while maintaining compliance with platform policies and seller agreements.

2. Types of Disputes

Disputes may arise under the following scenarios:

Item received is damaged or defective
Incorrect product delivered
Missing items from shipment
Package marked delivered but not received
Refund denied or delayed
Seller non-compliance or misrepresentation

Each type requires verification and evidence before resolution.

3. Reporting Timeline

Customers must report issues within defined timeframes:

Damaged or defective items: within 48 hours of delivery
Missing items: within 24–48 hours
Incorrect items: within return window
Non-delivery: within 7 days of expected delivery date

Late reporting may result in rejection of the claim.

4. Evidence Requirements

To validate disputes, customers may be required to provide:

Images of the product and packaging
Unboxing videos (for high-value items)
Order details and invoice
Description of the issue

Failure to provide sufficient evidence may lead to denial.

5. Resolution Options

Depending on the case, the following resolutions may be offered:

Replacement of the item
Partial refund
Full refund
Return and refund

The final decision is based on policy eligibility and investigation findings.

6. Marketplace vs Platform Responsibility

Responsibility differs based on fulfillment type:

Platform-Fulfilled Orders: Platform handles resolution directly
Marketplace Orders: Seller may be involved in decision-making

In case of conflict, platform may intervene as a mediator.

7. Investigation Process

Once a dispute is raised:

Case is logged and assigned a reference ID
Initial review conducted within 24 hours
Seller/logistics partner contacted (if required)
Evidence evaluated
Final resolution communicated

Typical resolution time: 3–7 business days

8. Lost or Undelivered Packages

If an order is marked delivered but not received:

Customer must verify delivery location
Platform checks courier proof (GPS/photo/signature)
Investigation initiated

Outcomes:

Replacement issued
Refund processed
Claim denied if delivery proof is valid
9. Damaged or Defective Items

For items damaged in transit or defective:

Replacement is preferred over refund
Refund provided if replacement unavailable

Repeated claims from the same account may trigger additional verification.

10. Incorrect or Missing Items

If wrong or incomplete items are received:

Customer must report immediately
Return pickup may be arranged

Resolution depends on:

Stock availability
Seller confirmation
11. Refund Disputes

If a refund is denied or partially issued:

Customer may request re-evaluation
Case reviewed by senior support team

Final decisions are based strictly on policy compliance.

12. Escalation Process

If a customer is not satisfied with initial resolution:

Case can be escalated to higher support tier
Escalation must include justification

Escalation levels:

Level 1: Customer Support
Level 2: Senior Resolution Team
Level 3: Compliance Review Team
13. Policy Exception Handling

Certain requests fall outside standard policy, such as:

Requests to keep item and receive full refund
Claims without evidence
Requests beyond return window

In such cases:

Requests may be denied
Partial goodwill compensation may be offered (case-dependent)
14. Abuse and Fraud Prevention

The platform monitors suspicious activities such as:

Repeated false claims
Intentional misuse of return policies
Fake damage reports

Actions may include:

Claim rejection
Account suspension
Legal action in severe cases
15. Sensitive Data Handling

Customers must not share sensitive information such as:

Full payment details
OTPs or passwords

Support agents must redact such data if received.

16. Final Decision Authority

All dispute resolutions are subject to final review by the platform. Decisions are made based on:

Policy guidelines
Evidence submitted
Seller input

These decisions are binding.

17. Customer Communication

Customers are informed at every stage:

Acknowledgement of dispute
Status updates
Final resolution

Clear communication is maintained to ensure transparency.

18. Policy Updates

The platform reserves the right to update dispute handling policies as needed.

19. Customer Support

Customers may contact support via chat, email, or phone for dispute-related issues. Escalation requests are handled within 24–72 hours.

Appendix C: Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

A: A disagreement between customer, seller, or platform regarding an issue.

Q2: How can I escalate my issue?

A: By requesting escalation through customer support.

Q3: How long does dispute resolution take?

A: Typically 48–72 hours, depending on complexity.

Q4: Can I escalate multiple times?

A: Yes, but repeated escalations may be limited.

Q5: What happens during escalation?

A: Case is reviewed by higher-level support or compliance team.

Q6: Can seller decisions be overridden?

A: Yes, platform may intervene if policy violation is found.

Q7: What if I am not satisfied with resolution?

A: You can request further escalation.

Q8: Are disputes handled fairly?

A: Yes, based on evidence and policy guidelines.

Q9: Can disputes lead to account suspension?

A: Yes, in cases of abuse or fraud.

Q10: Is legal action necessary?

A: Usually not; most disputes are resolved internally.

Q11: What evidence is required?

A: Photos, order details, and communication records.

Q12: Can I dispute after refund?

A: Generally no, unless new issue arises.

Q13: What if seller is unresponsive?

A: Platform will intervene.

Q14: Are all disputes accepted?

A: No, invalid or late claims may be rejected.
